# Cold WhatsApp — only when you have a legitimate reason to have the number

Cold WhatsApp in Nigeria is the bluntest tool in the kit. It's also the most likely to get your number reported. Use it only when:

1. **The number is publicly published** by the contact — on a real estate listing, on a hostel sign, on a business card, in a real estate WhatsApp group with public visibility.
2. **You have a specific reason** to contact them — you saw their listing, you got the number from a public listing site, a mutual contact mentioned them.
3. **You name the source in your first line.** This is non-negotiable. Without it you look like spam.

If none of those conditions hold, use LinkedIn or email instead. Don't message strangers cold.

## Differences vs the warm WhatsApp templates

| | Warm (`/whatsapp/messages.md`) | Cold (this file) |
|---|---|---|
| Recipient | Knows you, friend-of-friend, or someone you've previously messaged | Stranger |
| First line | "Hi [Name] 👋" | "Hi [Name], I got your number from **[source]** — apologies for the cold message" |
| Ask | Direct (try the app, send a referral) | Soft (open question, no immediate ask) |
| Frequency | Up to 2 messages | 1 message + 1 follow-up max |

If the cold contact replies, switch to the warm template register for follow-up.

## Cold WhatsApp opener (single message — don't double-tap with multiple)

### After seeing a real estate listing (theirs or one they brokered)

> Hi **[Name]**, saw your listing for **[Property name / area]** on **[platform]**. I'm building Property360 — software for Nigerian landlords and agents managing rent, leases, and tenants in one app. Wanted to ask: how do you currently track rent and lease docs for your portfolio?
>
> No pitch, just curious how the workflow looks today.
>
> — **[Your name]**

### After finding their number in a hostel sign / property listing

> Hi **[Name]**, I saw your number on the **[Hostel name / Property name]** listing. Apologies for the cold message. I'm building Property360 to help Nigerian hostel owners track rent, send invoices, and stop chasing payments. Wondering if you have 1 minute for a quick question about how you handle rent today?
>
> — **[Your name]**

### After getting a referral from a mutual contact

> Hi **[Name]**, **[Mutual contact]** mentioned you run **[Agency / Hostel / Portfolio]** and suggested I reach out. I'm building Property360 — software for Nigerian landlords and property managers. Mind if I send you a one-pager?
>
> — **[Your name]**

**Why these work:** The first line earns the right to be there. You either (a) cite a public source they themselves published, or (b) name the mutual contact. Anything else is spam.

---

## Follow-up #1 (4–5 days later, only if no reply)

> Hi **[Name]** — sorry to bother twice. Closing the loop: if it's not relevant, just say "not now" and I'll stop. If it is, here's a quick look at what Property360 does: https://property360.africa
>
> Thanks for your time either way.

If they don't reply to follow-up #1: **stop**. Three cold WhatsApps to a non-replier is harassment in this market.

---

## What to do when they reply

| Reply | Your move |
|---|---|
| "Not interested" | Reply: "No problem, thanks for getting back to me. If anything changes, you know where to find me." End thread. Don't argue. |
| "Send me details" | Reply with a short 3-line summary + the `/for-agencies` link (for agencies) or `property360.africa` (for landlords/hostels). Don't dump 5 messages. |
| "Tell me more" | Offer a call. "Happy to do a 10-min walkthrough on a call — when's good for you this week?" |
| "What does it cost?" | Reply with the Solo price + 14-day free trial. Don't hide pricing. |
| A specific question about a feature | Answer it directly. Don't deflect to a demo. |

## How to ask for numbers (so you don't have to cold-WhatsApp strangers)

The best cold WhatsApp number is one you got from a *warm* introduction. Two simple lines that work:

**To a current customer:**
> "If you know other landlords or hostel owners who could use this, would you be open to introducing me? Even one name + their WhatsApp would help me a ton."

**To a non-customer who didn't sign up but was polite:**
> "Totally fair. Quick ask — do you know anyone else in property management I should reach out to? Just trying to figure out who'd find this useful."

People you've already spoken to are 10× more likely to give you 1–2 names than to onboard themselves. Mining warm contacts for cold-but-warmed numbers is the highest-ROI move you can make in your first 90 days.
