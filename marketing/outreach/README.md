# Cold outreach — templates per channel

For when you don't have an existing relationship with the contact. (For warm outreach to people who know you, see `../whatsapp/messages.md`.)

## Files

- [`linkedin.md`](linkedin.md) — LinkedIn connection requests + first DM + follow-up. Highest signal channel for property managers and agency owners.
- [`email.md`](email.md) — Cold email subject lines + body templates + 2-step follow-up sequence. Lower reply rates than LinkedIn but creates a written audit trail.
- [`whatsapp-cold.md`](whatsapp-cold.md) — Cold WhatsApp guidance + 3 opener variants, only for use when you have a legitimate public reason to have the number. Different (softer) register than the warm WhatsApp templates.

## Suggested cadence (solo founder, ~2 hours/day on outreach)

Daily mix:

- **20 LinkedIn connection requests** (5 minutes per lead to research, 60 minutes total)
- **20 cold emails** (mostly templated, 30 minutes)
- **5–10 cold WhatsApps** to publicly-published numbers (20 minutes)
- **2–3 follow-ups** on yesterday's threads (10 minutes)
- **Track everything in a spreadsheet** (10 minutes)

This is enough to fill a 4–6 week growth push. After that, your reply rates tell you which channel to double down on.

## Order to try a single lead

If you have a high-value lead (a big agency, a hostel with 100+ rooms), don't pick one channel — sequence them across 2–3 weeks. The combined touch is far more memorable than three on the same channel.

1. **Day 0** — LinkedIn connection request (with note).
2. **Day 1** — If they accept, LinkedIn first DM. If they don't, skip to step 3.
3. **Day 4** — Cold email.
4. **Day 9** — Email follow-up #1.
5. **Day 12** — If you have a publicly-listed phone number AND haven't received a reply, one cold WhatsApp.
6. **Day 19** — Email follow-up #2 (close-the-loop).
7. **Stop** if no reply by day 22.

Stop means stop. The follow-up budget is finite. Burning it on dead leads costs you the leads who actually would have replied.

## Anti-spam rules across all channels

- **Always name the source.** Why you're reaching out, where you found them, who suggested them.
- **Never copy-paste a template wholesale.** Always change at least the second sentence to reference something specific to the lead.
- **Never attach files in the first cold message.** Link instead.
- **Don't send weekends or after 8pm.** Even "always-on" Nigerian operators read cold outreach worse outside business hours.
- **Reply rate &lt; 5% means the channel or audience is wrong.** Don't push harder. Switch channels or switch audiences.
