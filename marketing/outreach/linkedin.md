# LinkedIn — cold outreach to property managers and agency owners

LinkedIn is the highest-signal cold channel for Nigerian property managers and agency principals. People list their job title, agency name, and city in plain sight — so you can target precisely. Reply rates are 2–4× higher than email for this audience.

## Who to target (and how to find them)

In LinkedIn search:

- **Job title filters**: `property manager`, `head of property management`, `real estate broker`, `principal partner` + `hostel`, `student accommodation`, `facility manager`
- **Location filter**: Nigeria (then narrow to specific cities — Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt, Enugu, Ibadan, Calabar — based on where you can support customers in person)
- **Company filter**: any agency with "property", "real estate", or "facilities" in its name and 1–50 employees (the >50 group are usually big enough to have built their own tools)

Aim for **20 connection requests per day**. LinkedIn caps weekly invites at ~100; don't exceed it or your account gets restricted. Always include a note with the request.

---

## Connection request note (300-char limit on LinkedIn)

Pick the audience that fits the lead.

### To a property manager / agency owner

> Hi **[Name]**, came across your work managing properties in **[city]**. I'm building Property360 — software for Nigerian property managers. Would love to connect and learn how your operation works today.

### To a hostel owner

> Hi **[Name]**, saw you run **[Hostel name]**. I'm building Property360 to help Nigerian hostel owners manage rent and tenants in one place. Would love to connect and pick your brain on the workflow.

### To a small landlord (less common on LinkedIn, but useful for portfolio owners)

> Hi **[Name]**, noticed you manage a rental portfolio in **[city]**. I'm building Property360 — software for Nigerian landlords. Open to connecting?

**Why this works:** It's a research-shaped opening, not a sales pitch. Reply rate is high because there's no ask. The pitch comes after they accept.

## Follow-up DM after they accept (send within 24 hours)

### To a property manager / agency owner

> Thanks for connecting, **[Name]**! Quick question — how are you currently handling rent collection and tenant communication across your landlords? Most agencies I speak to use a mix of WhatsApp, spreadsheets, and trust. Curious where your bottleneck sits.
>
> The reason I ask — I built Property360 for exactly this. One workspace across all the landlords you manage, with per-property permission flags so each landlord keeps ownership. There's a one-pager you can forward to your team:
>
> https://property360.africa/for-agencies
>
> Happy to do a 15-min walkthrough on a call too if it's interesting.

### To a hostel owner

> Thanks for accepting, **[Name]**! Quick one — how do you currently track rent across the rooms? Most hostel owners I've spoken to are still using a paper book or a WhatsApp group, and the 1st of the month is brutal.
>
> Property360 sends every tenant an invoice automatically, lets them pay online, and shows you the status of every room in one dashboard. Free for 14 days, no card. Worth a look?
>
> https://property360.africa

### To a small landlord

> Thanks for connecting **[Name]**. Quick one — are you collecting rent through WhatsApp + bank transfer like most landlords here? Property360 turns that mess into one app. Tenants pay through Paystack, you get notified instantly, and the lease lives in the app so it's never lost.
>
> Free for 14 days: https://property360.africa

## Follow-up #2 (5–7 days after the first DM, if no reply)

Keep it short, give them an easy out, don't push pricing.

> Hey **[Name]** — circling back in case the first message got buried. No pressure either way; if it's not relevant just say "not now" and I'll close the loop. If it is interesting, here's the link again: https://property360.africa

**Rule:** If they don't reply to follow-up #2, **stop**. Send them the WhatsApp warm template only when they've shown interest somewhere else (commented on a post, opened a free trial, etc).

---

## Engagement before pitching (advanced)

Beats cold DM every time. Spend 5 minutes per lead doing one of these *before* you send the connection request:

1. **Like + comment** on their last 1–2 posts. Real comments (not "Great post!" — actually say what you found useful). Take 2 days, then send the connection request with a reference to what you commented on.
2. **Share their post** with a thoughtful quote of one line they wrote. Tag them. Then send the connection request the next day.
3. **Reply to a comment they left on someone else's post.** This pulls you into their notifications without spam.

Doing this on 5 high-value leads per week (large agency owners, big hostel operators) is worth more than 50 cold connection requests to small fish.

---

## Don't

- ❌ Don't pitch in the connection request. The whole point of a 2-step opener is room to breathe.
- ❌ Don't open with a video or voice note as the first message — too much commitment from someone who barely knows you. Voice/video earns its place at message #2 or #3.
- ❌ Don't copy-paste — recipient can spot a templated message at 10 paces. Edit the second sentence to reference something specific from their profile or their company.
- ❌ Don't pitch to their personal page if their company has a LinkedIn page run by someone else. Aim for decision makers.
- ❌ Don't use "I noticed you don't have…" framing. It implies a deficiency. Use "I noticed you do X" + offer to make X easier.
