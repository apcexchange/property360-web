# WhatsApp messages — ready to send

5 copy-and-paste messages, each tuned for a different audience. Personal customisation in **[brackets]**.

Rules of thumb:
- Use the recipient's first name. Nigerian WhatsApp is informal; using the name lifts reply rates a lot.
- Send one message, not three. If they read but don't reply, follow up after 3–5 days, not the same day.
- Keep the link as plain text — WhatsApp auto-detects it and shows a preview card from the landing page.
- Don't paste this whole document. Pick the one that fits, swap the brackets, send.

## Tips before you send

- **Voice notes beat text** for cold outreach. A 30-second voice note saying the same thing converts at 2–4× the rate of typed copy. Try it on at least 5 cold contacts a week.
- **Send between 7–10am or 8–10pm.** Replies during the workday are weaker; people skim and forget.
- **Don't mass-send.** WhatsApp throttles spammers and Nigerian recipients can spot bulk messages. 10–20 personalised messages a day beats 200 broadcasts.
- **Track replies in a spreadsheet** — name, segment, did they sign up, did they add a property. The numbers from this become the traction you quote to investors.
